Interactive Reference Guide

▶ What is a Covalent Bond?

A covalent bond forms when two non-metal atoms share a pair of valence electrons. Each atom contributes one electron to the shared pair. The goal is to achieve a stable electron configuration — typically 8 electrons (an octet) in the outer shell, or 2 (a duet) for hydrogen.

Electrons that aren't shared become lone pairs, which still influence the molecule's shape even though they're not bonds.

▶ VSEPR Theory

VSEPR (Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion) predicts molecular shape. Electron groups — both bonding pairs and lone pairs — repel each other and arrange to maximize distance.

Four groups → tetrahedral arrangement. But the molecular shape depends on how many of those groups are lone pairs vs. bonding pairs.

▶ Polarity

A polar molecule has an uneven distribution of charge. This happens when (1) the bonds are polar (atoms have different electronegativities) AND (2) the molecular shape is asymmetric, so the dipoles don't cancel.

Water is polar — bent shape means dipoles don't cancel. Methane is non-polar — symmetric tetrahedral shape means dipoles cancel perfectly.

▶ Intermolecular Forces

IMFs are attractions between molecules (not within). Strength order: London dispersion (weakest) < dipole-dipole < hydrogen bonding (strongest for small molecules).

Water has hydrogen bonding (strong) → bp 100°C. Methane only has London dispersion forces (weak) → bp −162°C.

▶ Why Structure Matters

The central idea: Atoms → Bonds → Shape → Polarity → IMFs → Physical Properties.

Every macroscopic property (boiling point, state, solubility) is determined by molecular structure. H₂O and CH₄ have similar mass (~18 g/mol vs ~16 g/mol), but their structures create a 262°C gap in boiling points.

▶ Fun Facts & Mind-Blowers
🌡️Without hydrogen bonds, water would boil at −40°C — colder than a Canadian winter.
🪐Saturn's moon Titan has liquid methane lakes at −179°C. It rains methane there.
🧊Ice floats because hydrogen bonds create an open lattice less dense than liquid water.
📊The boiling point gap between H₂O and CH₄ is 262°C — all because of molecular shape.
🔬Your body is about 60% water by mass — roughly 42 liters for an average person.
🌍Methane traps 80× more heat than CO₂ over 20 years — making it a climate supervillain.
